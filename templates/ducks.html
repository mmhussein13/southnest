Duck Farming

Duck farming is a profitable agricultural enterprise that involves raising ducks primarily for meat, eggs, or feathers. Ducks are highly adaptable and can be raised in various environments, making them an excellent choice for both small-scale and commercial farming operations.

Benefits of Duck Farming

  • Ducks are more disease resistant compared to chickens
  • They require less maintenance and care
  • Ducks can be raised on land or water
  • They are excellent foragers and natural pest controllers
  • Duck eggs and meat command premium prices in the market

Popular Duck Breeds

Several duck breeds are commonly raised for different purposes:

Pekin Duck

A fast-growing breed with white feathers and orange beak and feet. Pekin ducks are primarily raised for meat production and can reach market weight in just 7-8 weeks.

Muscovy Duck

Known for their lean meat and excellent flavor. Muscovy ducks are also good egg producers and are more heat-tolerant than other breeds.

Khaki Campbell

An excellent egg-laying breed that can produce up to 340 eggs per year. They have a khaki-colored plumage and are known for their foraging abilities.

Indian Runner

Known for their upright posture and excellent egg-laying capacity. Indian Runners are active foragers and are often kept for pest control in gardens.

Duck Farming Guide

Starting a duck farming operation requires careful planning. Here's a basic guide to help you get started:

Housing Requirements

Ducks need protection from predators and harsh weather. A simple shelter with adequate ventilation and protection from rain is sufficient. The floor should be covered with clean bedding material like straw or wood shavings.

Water Access

While ducks don't necessarily need a pond to swim in, they do need access to water deep enough to submerge their heads. This helps them clean their nostrils and eyes.

Feeding

Ducks can be fed commercial duck feed supplemented with greens, vegetables, and insects. They are excellent foragers and will find a significant portion of their food if allowed to free-range.

Health Management

Regular health checks, clean housing, and proper nutrition are key to maintaining healthy ducks. Look out for signs of illness such as lethargy, loss of appetite, or unusual droppings.
